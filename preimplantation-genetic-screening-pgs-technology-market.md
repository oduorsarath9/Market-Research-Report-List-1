<p><p>Preimplantation Genetic Screening (PGS) technology involves various methods to analyze genetic material from embryos before implantation. Next-generation sequencing (NGS) allows comprehensive genetic profiling by sequencing millions of fragments concurrently, identifying genetic anomalies. Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) amplifies DNA to detect specific genetic sequences with high sensitivity. Fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) uses fluorescent probes to visualize specific chromosome abnormalities. Comparative genomic hybridization (CGH) assesses variations in DNA content, while single-nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) analysis identifies variations at single genetic locations, aiding in embryo selection for healthy implantation.</p></p>
