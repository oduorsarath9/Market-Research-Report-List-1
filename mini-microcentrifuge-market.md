<p><p>The Mini Microcentrifuge market is divided into two primary types: Refrigeratable and Non-refrigerable types. Refrigeratable microcentrifuges are designed to maintain low temperatures, preserving samples sensitive to heat, making them ideal for biological research and clinical applications. Non-refrigerable types, on the other hand, operate at ambient temperatures, focusing on general laboratory functions where temperature control is less critical. Both types cater to different needs in research and diagnostics, reflecting the diverse requirements of laboratories and scientific tasks.</p></p>
