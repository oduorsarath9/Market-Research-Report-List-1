<p><p>A Pressurized Water Reactor (PWR) is a type of nuclear reactor that uses water as both a coolant and a neutron moderator. In this system, water is kept under high pressure to prevent it from boiling, allowing it to absorb heat from the nuclear fission process and transfer this heat to a secondary loop, where steam is generated to drive turbines for electricity production. PWRs are the most common type of nuclear reactors worldwide, favored for their safety and reliability.</p><p>The Pressurized Water Reactor (PWR) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.1% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing demand for clean and sustainable energy sources, as well as ongoing advancements in nuclear technology that enhance safety and efficiency. Moreover, governments around the globe are focusing on reducing carbon emissions, leading to renewed interest in nuclear power as a viable alternative to fossil fuels. Recent trends indicate a shift towards modernization of existing PWR plants, investments in small modular reactors, and enhanced regulatory frameworks, all contributing to a robust market outlook for PWR technology.</p></p>
