<p><p>High-Tc superconductors are categorized primarily into two market types: 1G HTS and 2G HTS. </p><p>1G HTS refers to first-generation superconductors, typically comprised of thick films or wires, offering high performance but with limitations in scalability and cost. In contrast, 2G HTS incorporates advanced manufacturing techniques to produce thin films, resulting in enhanced performance, flexibility, and lower costs. These advancements are driving applications in power generation, transmission, and medical imaging, contributing to a growing market driven by technological innovation and increasing demand.</p></p>
