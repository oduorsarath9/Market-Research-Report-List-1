<p><p>The Smart Card Integrated Circuit market is divided into two primary types: contact and contactless. Contact smart cards require physical connection to a reader via metal contacts, ensuring secure data exchange. They're commonly used in banking and identification. In contrast, contactless smart cards utilize radio frequency identification (RFID) technology, allowing for data transfer without direct contact. This enhances convenience and speed, making them popular for public transportation and access control systems. Both types serve critical roles in enhancing security and efficiency in various applications.</p></p>
